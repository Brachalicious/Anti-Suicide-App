1. Who we are

Fight Suicide Project by MysticMinded³³ (“Fight Suicide Project,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) is a free, faith-friendly mental-wellness companion app created by an independent developer. We provide self-help tools, a private diary, mood tracking, breathing and grounding exercises, a personalized safety plan, curated crisis resources, and optional AI-assisted chat.

We are not a healthcare provider, hospital, clinic, therapist, or crisis service. We are a wellness app. For emergencies, always use the crisis resources above.

2. What information we collect

2a. Information you provide directly

  • Google account info when you sign in with Google: your name, email address, and profile photo URL (provided by Google's OAuth, not entered by you).
  • Display name you optionally set in Account Settings.
  • Diary entries, mood logs, songs/notes you save, and safety-plan answers — anything you choose to type into the app.
  • Chatbot messages you send to the in-app chat companions (Help Guide, Mom/Dad/Pet, MysticMinded).
  • Your Google Gemini API key, if you choose to enable AI replies by pasting your own key. This is stored only on your device — see Section 5.

2b. Information collected automatically

  • Firebase Authentication identifier (UID) — a random ID Google assigns to your account so we can save your data securely.
  • Local browser storage (localStorage) — your app preferences (display name, sound/animation toggles, last-used tabs, etc.) live on your device only.
  • Basic technical metadata that any web app receives from your browser (IP address, browser type, timestamps) — handled by our hosting provider Netlify; we do not log or analyze it.

2c. What we do NOT collect

  • ❌ Your contacts, photos, microphone, or camera (unless you explicitly use an in-app feature that requests them, e.g. the optional voice-recorder for Guided Meditation).
  • ❌ Your location.
  • ❌ Advertising identifiers — there are no ads in this app.
  • ❌ Any health data from Apple Health or Google Health Connect.

3. How we use your information

We use your data only to:

  • Let you sign in and recognize you across devices.
  • Save and sync your diary, mood logs, safety plan, and preferences so they're there when you come back.
  • Personalize the app (greet you by name, show your data not someone else's).
  • Pass your chatbot messages to Google's Gemini AI only when you have explicitly enabled AI replies (see Section 5).
We do not sell your data. We do not share it with advertisers, data brokers, insurance companies, employers, or any third parties — full stop. No targeted ads. No analytics tracking your behavior across the web.

4. Where your data lives

  • Account, diary, mood logs, safety plan, settings → stored in Google Firebase / Firestore (US data centers), encrypted in transit (HTTPS) and at rest.
  • App preferences → stored locally in your device's browser storage. Clearing your browser data clears these.
  • Your Gemini API key → stored locally on your device only. We never see it, never store it on our servers, and never log it.

5. AI features (Google Gemini)

Three of our chat companions can use Google's Gemini AI to give thoughtful replies. This is OFF by default.

To turn it on, you can paste your own free Google Gemini API key in ⚙️ Account Settings. Once enabled:

  • Each chat message you send is transmitted directly from your browser to Google's Generative Language API using your own API key.
  • Your messages and the AI's replies are not stored on our servers — they live only in your current chat window unless you save them yourself.
  • Google's own terms govern how they process your messages. Read them at policies.google.com/privacy and ai.google.dev/gemini-api/terms.
  • AI responses are machine-generated and are not reviewed by a human, are not medical advice, and are not a substitute for professional care.

6. Who can see your data

  • You. That's it. Each user can only read and write their own data, enforced by Firebase Security Rules.
  • Google hosts the data on our behalf as our infrastructure provider (Firebase). They cannot use it for their own purposes.
  • Law enforcement only if we receive a valid legal order, in which case we will comply as required by law.

7. Your rights (GDPR, CCPA, and just being a decent app)

You always have the right to:

  • Access your data — use ⚙️ Account Settings → 📥 Download My Data to get a JSON file of everything you've saved.
  • Correct your data — edit any diary entry, mood log, safety plan field, or display name in the app.
  • Delete your data — use ⚙️ Account Settings → 🗑️ Permanently Delete My Account. This wipes your Firestore record, deletes your Firebase Auth account, and clears local storage.
  • Withdraw consent — sign out at any time. Disable AI by removing your API key from Account Settings.
  • Complain to your local data-protection authority (e.g. EU Data Protection Authorities, California Privacy Protection Agency).

8. Children

This app is intended for users aged 13 and older. If you are under 16 and live in the European Economic Area, please ask a parent or guardian for consent before using the app. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If you believe we have, please contact us and we will delete it.

9. Data retention

We keep your data for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account, all your data is removed from our active databases immediately. Encrypted backups may persist for up to 30 days before being permanently overwritten.

10. International users

Our servers (Firebase) are located in the United States. By using this app, you consent to the transfer and storage of your data in the US, which may not have the same data-protection laws as your country.

11. Security

We use industry-standard safeguards: HTTPS encryption for all data in transit, encrypted-at-rest storage via Firebase, OAuth 2.0 for authentication, and per-user Firestore security rules so you can never accidentally see (or be seen by) another user. No system is 100% secure, but we take this seriously, especially because of the sensitive nature of mental-health data.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. If we make significant changes, we'll notify you in-app the next time you open it. The "Last updated" date at the top will always reflect the latest version.
